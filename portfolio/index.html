UGA CAES keyboard_arrow_down
As lead font-end developer for the University of Georgia College of Agricultural & Environmental Sciences, I was responsible for implementing a mock up from a designer into a fully functional web template, and then subsequently assiting in the conversion of page elements into JSP templates for use in the Adobe Experience Manager CMS. The rest of my time was spent designing and developing advanced components that would dynamically pull in news, events, images, and other forms of data to make the content manager's job as simple as possible.

In this case, I was responsible both for the initial UI design as well as the subsequent development of this template. What template and navigational structure could accomodate a site with four pages as well as a site with four hundred? Walk Georgia keyboard_arrow_down
I was hired by the University of Georgia to do a complete design overhaul of the Walk Georgia program in partnership with the Coca-Cola Foundation. In addition to the design and development work, our team of two was responsible for a technical overhaul of the fitness tracking system. We developed a conversion formula that could accurately compare unrelated activities (yoga and swimming) and then award points to the user based on time, difficulty, etc. After being promoted, it was then my responsibility to assit in hiring another designer to maintain and continue the program.

Note: due to the internet policy of the University of Georgia, I am unable to share the repository for this project.
